# What is CPM in Podcasting

**CPM (Cost Per Mille) in podcasting is the advertising metric that represents the cost an advertiser pays for one thousand listens to, or downloads of, an episode containing their ad.**

In podcast advertising, CPM is the standard unit for buying and selling ad inventory. The "Mille" in CPM is Latin for thousand, so a $25 CPM means an advertiser pays $25 for every 1,000 times the episode with their ad is downloaded or listened to, as measured by the podcast host or an independent tracking service. It is a pre-negotiated, fixed rate, making it a primary model for host-read ads and dynamically inserted advertisements within podcast content. This differs from performance-based models like affiliate marketing, where payment is tied to a specific listener action.

CPM rates vary significantly, typically ranging from $15 to $50+, influenced by factors like the podcast's niche, audience size (downloads per episode), host credibility, and production quality. A highly-targeted, niche show with an engaged audience often commands a higher CPM than a broad-audience show with similar download numbers. Advertisers also distinguish between *download CPM* (based on the episode download) and *listener CPM* (based on confirmed ad listens via tracking), with the latter often being more valuable.

**Why it matters:** CPM is the fundamental currency of the podcast advertising industry. For podcasters, it directly determines their revenue potential and provides a clear metric to value their show to potential sponsors. For advertisers, it offers a predictable and scalable way to budget for campaigns and compare the relative cost-efficiency of different podcasts. A high CPM isn't inherently good or bad; it reflects the perceived value and engagement of the audience.

**Use cases:** A personal finance podcast with 50,000 downloads per episode and a $30 CPM would earn $1,500 for a single ad slot ($30 x [50,000/1,000]). An advertiser for a B2B software tool might pay a premium CPM of $45 to a smaller, niche tech podcast because its 10,000 highly-targeted listeners are more likely to convert than a general audience of 100,000.

**Related terms:** **RPM (Revenue Per Mille)** is the companion metric for the podcaster, representing their actual earnings per thousand downloads after platform fees or revenue shares. **Dynamic Ad Insertion (DAI)** is the technology that allows ads to be inserted into podcast episodes post-publication, often sold on a CPM basis. **Impressions** refer to the total number of times an ad is delivered, which the CPM rate is applied to.
